Salade Niçoise

Description:

Salad Niçoise is a French salad that originated in the Nice region of France in the 19th century.
It typically consists of mixed greens, eggs, Niçoise olives, lots of fresh veggies, and anchovies and/or tuna.
Salad Niçoise can be served as a tossed or composed salad.

Ingredients:

  • Half pound new potatoes, quartered
  • One Third pound fresh green beans, rinced, trimmed, and blanched
  • 5 Ounces of canned tuna
  • Half an onion, thinly sliced
  • Quarter cup pitted niçoise olives
  • Quarter cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Half pound chopped salad greens
  • One cup lemon vinaigrette
  • 3 Hard-boiled eggs, quartered
  • 3 Plum tomatoes, thinly sliced
  • 4 Anchovy filets
  • 1 Tablespoon capers

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil; add potatoes and cook until tender but still firm, about 15 minutes. Drain and cool.
  2. Combine potatoes, green beans, tuna, onion, parsley, olives, and parsley in a large bowl; refrigerate for 2 to 4 hours.
  3. Toss greens with vinaigrette in a salad bowl; top with chilled potato mixture. Garnish with eggs, tomatoes, anchovies, and capers.
  4. Enjoy!
